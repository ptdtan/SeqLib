1 /* rudp - (semi) reliable UDP communication. This adds an
2  * acknowledgement and resend layer on top of UDP.
3  *
4  * UDP is a packet based rather than stream based internet communication
5  * protocol. Messages sent by UDP are checked for integrety by the UDP layer,
6  * and discarded if transmission errors are detected. However packets are
7  * not necessarily received in the same order that they are sent,
8  * and packets may be duplicated or lost.
9 
10  * Using rudp packets are only very rarely lost, and the sender is
11  * notified if they are. After rudp there are still duplicate
12  * packets that may arrive out of order. Aside from the duplicates
13  * the packets are in order though.
14  *
15  * For many, perhaps most applications, TCP/IP is a saner choice
16  * than UDP or rudp. If the communication channel is between just
17  * two computers you can pretty much just treat TCP/IP as a fairly
18  * reliable pipe. However if the communication involves many
19  * computers sometimes UDP can be a better choice. It is possible to
20  * do broadcast and multicast with UDP but not with TCP/IP. Also
21  * for systems like parasol, where a server may be making and breaking
22  * connections rapidly to thousands of computers, TCP paradoxically
23  * can end up less reliable than UDP. Though TCP is relatively
24  * robust when a connection is made, it can relatively easily fail
25  * to make a connection in the first place, and spend quite a long
26  * time figuring out that the connection can't be made. Moreover at
27  * the end of each connection TCP goes into a 'TIMED_WAIT' state, which
28  * prevents another connection from coming onto the same port for a
29  * time that can be as long as 255 seconds. Since there are only
30  * about 15000 available ports, this limits TCP/IP to 60 connections
31  * per second in some cases. Generally the system does not handle
32  * running out of ports gracefully, and this did occur with the
33  * TCP/IP based version of Parasol.
34  *
35  * This module puts a thin layer around UDP to make it a little bit more
36  * reliable. Currently the interface is geared towards Parasol rather
37  * than broadcast type applications. This module will try to send
38  * a message a limited number of times before giving up. It puts
39  * a small header containing a message ID and timestamp on each message.
40  * This header is echoed back in acknowledgment by the reciever. This
41  * echo lets the sender know if it needs to resend the message, and
42  * lets it know how long a message takes to get to the destination and
43  * back. It uses this round trip time information to figure out how
44  * long to wait between resends.
45  *
46  * Much of this code is based on the 'Adding Reliability to a UDP Application
47  * section in volume I, chapter 20, section 5, of _UNIX Network Programming_
48  * by W. Richard Stevens. */
