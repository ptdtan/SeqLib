72 /* Calculate the melting temperature of substr(seq, start, length) using the
73  * formula from Bolton and McCarthy, PNAS 84:1390 (1962) as presented in
74  * Sambrook, Fritsch and Maniatis, Molecular Cloning, p 11.46 (1989, CSHL
75  * Press).
76  *
77  * Tm = 81.5 + 16.6(log10([Na+])) + .41*(%GC) - 600/length
78  *
79  * Where [Na+] is the molar sodium concentration, (%GC) is the percent of Gs
80  * and Cs in the sequence, and length is the length of the sequence.
81  *
82  * A similar formula is used by the prime primer selection program in GCG
83  * (http://www.gcg.com), which instead uses 675.0 / length in the last term
84  * (after F. Baldino, Jr, M.-F. Chesselet, and M.E. Lewis, Methods in
85  * Enzymology 168:766 (1989) eqn (1) on page 766 without the mismatch and
86  * formamide terms). The formulas here and in Baldino et al. assume Na+ rather
87  * than K+. According to J.G. Wetmur, Critical Reviews in BioChem. and
88  * Mol. Bio. 26:227 (1991) 50 mM K+ should be equivalent in these formulae to .2
89  * M Na+.
90  *
91  * This function takes salt_conc to be the millimolar (mM) concentration,
92  * since mM is the usual units in PCR applications. */
93
